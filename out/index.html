K-Collusion Index

Korean consumer price distortion signal

A composite dashboard that combines Numbeo cost-of-living indicators with OECD price-level and CPI concepts to flag where Korean consumer prices look unusually pressured.

This index does not legally determine collusion. It is a reference signal that combines international prices, purchasing power, inflation pressure, and competition-risk proxies.

Formula and limits

The score is an analytical weighting model for price distortion signals.

35%

Numbeo relative price

Cost of living, groceries, restaurant, and rent pressure relative to Korea

25%

OECD PPP price level

Purchasing-power-parity price-level deviation to reduce exchange-rate distortion

20%

OECD CPI pressure

Recent category inflation pressure and accumulated CPI movement

15%

Affordability burden

Local Purchasing Power and disposable-income context for consumer burden

5%

Competition risk proxy

Supplemental signal for concentration, import barriers, and distribution opacity
